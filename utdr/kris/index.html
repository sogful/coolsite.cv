Kris Dreemurr is a human Lightner and the main protagonist of Deltarune. They are the first hero as foretold in the Prophecy. Kris lives together with their adoptive mother, Toriel, at the Dreemurr Residence in northern Hometown.

In a Dark World, Kris functions as the party's leader. Kris excels at ACTing in battles, and their ACTs can have varying effects. While in a battle, Kris wields a sword, and can summon a shield when DEFENDing. In Chapter 4, during the cutscene where Kris parries the Sound of Justice's attack, it is revealed that the sword and shield can be wielded simultaneously.

For the majority of the game, Kris is controlled by the player. The few exceptions are brief cutscenes, or when they tear the SOUL out of their body.

Profile

Appearance

Kris is a human teenager with amber-colored skin and shoulder-length brown hair, with a couple strands sticking out slightly at the back of their head. Kris's long bangs are somewhat messy and are mainly swept to the left, which also casts a shadow over their eyes. Kris's eyes are occasionally visibly red, and can glow red in the dark - one of their eyes is also briefly red when they descend into a Dark World. Kris wears a brown collared, long-sleeve lime shirt with a horizontal yellow stripe, and nondescript brown pants and shoes.

In a Dark World, Kris's skin becomes cyan-colored, and their hair changes to navy. Kris's clothes are also replaced with silver armor, which makes them resemble a knight. This includes a set of shoulder guards, a chestplate, gloves, and boots. Kris gains a navy blue bodysuit underneath the set, and a pink mantle[54] drapes across their right shoulder. The shading for their skin is also used for their mantle, which gives the illusion of blue stripes in a few of their sprites.

To the player, Kris consistently has a blank, neutral expression. They seemingly stand straight during the majority of non-scripted actions.[55] Kris's body language is markedly different when they are about to remove or have removed the SOUL.

Personality

During the events of Deltarune, Kris is mentioned to be introverted.[56] Both Susie and King refer to Kris as a "quiet person," and Noelle is astonished if they want to talk about "anything."[57] After Kris's brother, Asriel, left for college, they grew distant from their few friends. Kris's side of their shared bedroom is also strikingly bare. The shared game console is underneath Asriel's bed, their old children's books are in Toriel's classroom instead, and there is nothing on their shelves. Toriel also mentions that Kris is not normally energetic. They have a history of not waking up on time, and often sleep through the start of class.[58] Kris also prefers someone else getting them to and from school.[59][60] It is implied that Kris enjoys thinking back on their childhood, and that they are unsettled by the thought of growing up.[61]

Contrary to Kris's apparent detachment, they do have a variety of interests. Kris has always been mischievous, frequently pulling pranks on Noelle such as hiding under her bed to scare her.[62] Kris also attempted to flush a bath bomb down the toilet at their home at one point, and Toriel is weary that they will try it again.[63] When they were younger, Kris studied the occult with Catti.[64] They appear to still have an interest in the topic, as one of their search queries was "how to use magic." Kris likes video games, having frequently played games with Asriel in their childhood,[65] and being good enough that they can seemingly defeat Berdly in competitive games.[66] They have also searched for tutorials on how to play video game songs on the piano.[67]

Kris enjoys playing the piano. Prior to the game, they frequently played the hospital piano,[68] and the receptionist believes they can play "beautifully."[69] If the player makes the SOUL wait in the kitchen of the Holiday residence after Kris closes the vent, Kris can be heard playing piano renditions of the Deltarune soundtrack. Kris seems upset by the player's inability to play the piano, as they look angry when Susie laughs at their bad playing while under the player's control,[70] and actively try to prevent saying that they'll never play again.[71][72]

Kris's favorite food is Toriel's pie,[73] though they seem to like sweet and especially chocolatey foods in general. They always take a piece from the Box of Heart Candy,[74] like hot chocolate[75] and are implied to fancy Choco Diamonds, as this consumable heals them more than any other party member.[76]

Kris is shown to have a significant amount of agency outside of the player's choices. This is most clearly shown in scenes where Kris rips the SOUL out of their body, usually trapping it in a confined space to prevent being followed. They are also able to influence some dialogue choices, occasionally, the player may choose an option that other characters note sounds like Kris is straining to say or that Kris outright refuses to say out loud.[77] Kris is also shown performing actions independently of the player's control, including protecting Susie from King or drinking juice with her in church, showing their friendships exist outside of the player's influence. Additionally, there are instances where Kris stops inspecting something to prevent the player from learning more: when inspecting Asriel's room in Queen's Mansion, Kris closes their eyes before seeing anything; when inspecting an article about Asgore's removal from the police force, Kris intentionally stops reading before the reason is given; and when turning the doorknob in the church, they refuse to open the door. At the end of Chapter 1, Red Big Mouth and Toriel both comment on Kris's physical appearance. They claim that Kris seems "a little sick" and does not "look quite [themselves]" respectively. This implies that being under the SOUL's control slightly changes how healthy Kris looks, and is outright confirmed to change their voice and behavior.[78]

The separation between the player and Kris is most clearly shown in the Weird Route, where it is heavily implied Kris's actions are being done by the player against their will. Noelle describes the voice she heard giving her orders as terrifying, noting Kris has been acting very strange lately. When Kris reunites with the party after defeating Berdly, Susie and Ralsei immediately notice Kris appears distraught. In Chapter 4, Kris gives the player multiple choices to abort the route which are directed at them specifically[79] and punishes the SOUL in a violent outburst if the route is continued. Kris also tries to remedy the player's actions by comforting Noelle and bringing Berdly to the hospital between the events of Chapter 3 and Chapter 4

Kris is noticeably distraught after defeating Spamton NEO in the normal route, and Susie notices Kris appearing to be in shock. If the player responds to Susie questioning if they're okay by saying "Yes," Kris appears strained in their response, making Susie think that they're not okay; if the player responds by saying "No," Kris yells out the response, concerning Susie and Ralsei. This heavily implies that Spamton's dialogue about being a puppet and cutting his strings to be free, combined with Spamton's ultimate fate, deeply unsettled Kris.

Main Story

History

Years prior to the events of Deltarune, Kris was adopted by the Dreemurrs and grew up in a loving family. However, as the only human child in Hometown, Kris often felt like an outsider. Kris once asked their adoptive parents when their "horns were going to grow in." In response, Toriel and Asgore bought a headband with red horns for Kris, who wore it for months.[80]

Growing up, Kris was very close with their older brother, Asriel. The two often spent time with their neighbors, December and Noelle, watching TV together and exploring the forests around town.[81] While the four were close friends, Kris and Noelle grew apart over the years. The cause for this is unknown. Kris had also wanted to befriend Bratty, as she was one of the "big kids." They willingly complied and retrieved food for her until she became sick.[82]

At some point, Toriel and Asgore divorced. While their family was broken, Kris and Asriel remained inseparable. On occasion, Asriel would buy Kris a hot chocolate at QC's Diner,[83] the place they used to frequent as a whole family.[84] One day, Asriel moved out to go to college, leaving Kris alone and without many friends, though they kept in contact before Hometown's internet outage.

Once Susie walks ahead, Kris can explore some of the hall, or can walk or try to outpace her.

When Susie reasons they should enter at the same time, Kris follows her into the closet. Inside, the two are unable to find the light switch, and they try to exit. The closet's door abruptly slams shut, and the papers fall. Both Kris and Susie back away from the falling paper before descending into the Closet Dark World.

Afterwards, Lancer interrupts Ralsei, who properly introduces himself and begins an encounter. Post battle, Lancer flees. If Kris walks through the empty town, Ralsei reminds them that they can run.

In the training area, Ralsei offers to teach Kris about battling via the Dummy. Kris can choose to agree or not, though either choice causes Ralsei to give them the Manual. Near the open Great Door, Ralsei believes that Kris's choices are important, and suggests that they do not FIGHT. Kris can reassure that they can handle it, or be silent before entering Card Kingdom.

As they venture the Field, Kris and Ralsei come across Lancer multiple times, and as well as his troops. Along the way, they discover that Susie attacked Lancer, consumed a Broken Cake, and attacked some Jigsawrys. Further east, Kris is unable to solve the Second Clock Puzzle due to a missing party member. The two find Susie nearby, and Ralsei reasons with her, including emphasizing that only Kris can seal a Dark Fountain.[87] She reluctantly joins, and the puzzle is solved.

The party finds C. Round blocking the nearby path. Ralsei advises Kris to include Susie in an ACT. However, C. Round gets knocked away and flees in one turn. Nearby Seam's Shop, Ralsei tells Kris that Darkners may need to be Warned about Susie. They find Lancer again, and Kris watches Susie try to scare him. Past the Warp Door, Kris is tasked with solving the two-box puzzle. Once it is solved, the party enters the Great Board.

At the halfway point, Lancer showcases what he learned from Susie, and realizes the party does not have a proper name. Lancer lets Kris choose the team name from the three papers in the bucket, since he assumes they do not care.[88] This choice transfers.

Round's healing, Kris is forced to ACT by bowing to make its crown fall off. Post battle, Lancer praises the three as heroes. However, Ralsei gently admonishes Susie, who then decides to join Lancer's team. Ralsei muses to Kris that he may have been harsh to her, and the two leave the area.

Throughout the Forest, Kris and Ralsei find Susie and Lancer, who proclaim themselves as a "DARK" version of the team name Kris chose. Kris watches Susie and Lancer's growing friendship and solves puzzles, including one in Clover's area. In the Bake Sale, Kris can also optionally purchase a Lancer Cookie from Susie and Lancer for 40 D$.

The two later find a disguised Lancer, who asks for help on creating a Thrash Machine to impress Susie. Kris and Ralsei comply, and the design choice transfers to Chapter 2. After navigating the Maze, Kris and Ralsei find an altered design that was created (or a replica of it), before Susie and Lancer begin an encounter. Upon winning, Susie once again rejoins the party, and Lancer tags along.

As the party's adventure goes on, Lancer grows uncomfortable with the situation and runs off towards Card Castle. Kris and the party attempt to follow him, but King's soldiers capture them.

Ralsei and Kris wake up in a prison cell together and quickly realize that Susie is being kept in an adjacent cell. Kris manages to guide Susie through a puzzle, unlocking her cell and allowing her to escape. After a confrontation with Lancer, Susie releases Kris and Ralsei from their cell.

After making their way up Card Castle, Kris and the party face off against King. After the fight, King deceives the party into believing he is willing to redeem himself, resulting in the party being cornered. Kris jumps in front of Susie to protect her, but is knocked back by another blow.

King approaches Kris and, after taunting them, lifts them into the air, ready to kill them. Regardless of how King was initially defeated, either Lancer and King's subjects rescue the party, or Susie distracts King long enough for Ralsei to cast Pacify on the King, putting him to sleep. Which outcome occurs is based on whether the party has harmed any of the denizens of the Dark World prior to facing King.

After saying goodbye to Ralsei, Kris seals the Eastern Fountain and returns home along with Susie. Kris can then explore Hometown and speak to the inhabitants. Once Kris returns home and goes to bed, the adventure is over.

At some point in the middle of the night, Kris tosses and turns in bed, only to suddenly leap from their covers and make their way into the center of their room. As they stand on the carpet, Kris slowly raises their arm before violently tearing out their SOUL from their body and forcefully tossing it into a cage in the corner of the room. The SOUL can be moved in the cage, indicating that the player controls the SOUL as opposed to Kris. Kris then brandishes a knife before turning their head and smiling menacingly, with their eye flashing red. The game then cuts to black.

Chapter 2

At the beginning of the Chapter, Toriel rushes up to Kris's room, having noticed a knife in the pie. She believes that Kris had decided to sneakily eat it whole while she was asleep. She laughs it off, though, suggesting she should lock the oven in the future before they both set off to school once again.

Later, Kris goes to school and enters the Dark World again with Susie. They meet Ralsei and explore Castle Town. Eventually, Susie mentions they have homework to do, prompting Ralsei to banish them from the Dark World until their homework is done.

They go to the Library, only to find another Dark World awaiting them, and jump into it, this time with Noelle and Berdly taking part in the adventure as well. They soon encounter Queen, who acts as the Chapter's main antagonist. She captures Noelle, while Berdly switches to her side voluntarily. Eventually, Kris splits up with the other party members and rescues Noelle.

In Kris's company, Noelle begins learning to be less timid and fearful. Later, after the entire party gets imprisoned in Queen's Mansion, Berdly learns to be less arrogant. The final boss fight has both parties control their respective mechas and do battle in the style of Punch-Out!!, with Kris and their friends ultimately emerging victorious. Kris then seals the Library Fountain, and the Lightners all return to the Light World.

After exploring Hometown, Kris goes home and Toriel invites Susie to spend the night at the Dreemurr residence. She sends the pair to wash their hands. When Kris goes to the bathroom, they rip out their SOUL as soon as they turn the water on and escape through the window.

Following a chat between Toriel and Susie, Kris returns to the bathroom, washes their hands and re-inserts the SOUL. It is later revealed that the tires on Toriel's car have been slashed. When Susie and Toriel are both asleep, Kris opens a Dark Fountain.

Relationships

Asriel

Asriel has been consistently kind to his younger sibling. He has used the knockoff controller to play video games,[89] begged for forgiveness for Kris's "sins,"[90] and had carried Kris to school. The duo are very close since childhood, and various Hometown residents acknowledge this.[83] After Asriel left for college, Kris often stayed up late conversing with him online, until the internet went out.[91] Despite this, Kris deeply misses Asriel.[92]

In Chapter 3, when the player implies they have seen Ralsei's face before, Kris will break into laughter while also assuring them that Ralsei and Asriel are different.[93] In Chapter 4, when Susie mistakes Asriel for Ralsei, Kris adamantly points out every difference between them.[94]

Toriel

Years prior to the events of Deltarune, it is implied Toriel had difficulties raising Kris, as she had frequently borrowed How To Care For A Human from the Library.[95] Despite this, Toriel is patient and motherly, and finds most of Kris's antics endearing. She still holds Kris's hand when taking them to school, and keeps their childhood drawing in her classroom. Although she loves both Kris and Asriel equally, she tries to limit Kris's impulsiveness, such as locking the chocolate kisses in her room.[96]

Susie

In Chapter 1, Kris is initially intimidated by Susie's rough demeanor. After Susie warns Kris and they escape the spade storm together, Kris is no longer terrified of her.[97] They observe Susie's friendliness towards Lancer, and she tries to recruit Kris for their "DARK" team.[98] However, after Susie promises not to hurt Lancer's father, she decides to listen to Kris's commands.[99] After the battle against King, Kris shields her from a spade attack, only to get knocked down by the King. Now seeing Kris as her friend, Susie stops King from killing them. Upon returning to school, she asks Kris if they can return to the Dark World with a friendly smile. When Toriel calls Kris afterwards, Kris tells her that they were hanging out with a friend, indicating that Kris had a good time with Susie, and Monster Kid's ridicule of Susie offends Kris after their adventure.[100]

During Chapter 2, more evidence of their friendship is shown through Kris's actions. They focus on Susie when they drink her Tea, and is distinctly compliant to Susie. If Kris inspects the "Susie-like statue" and "ICE-E statue," Susie wants the former, and insists Kris gets the latter for their room. Kris steals either or both without a prompt, even if the latter statue discomforts them.[101]

Throughout Chapters 3 and 4, Kris and Susie grow even closer. Kris is reassured by Susie's presence[102] and most moments during the Chapters when Kris's personality is shown are through their interactions with Susie, such as when Kris and Susie pour drinks into each other's mouths or when Kris lifts Susie without a prompt while she eats the snack tray outside Noelle's house. Kris is also shown to care a lot for Susie through the Sword Route, as Kris flinches away from the screen when the player directs them to kill HERO_AXE and, if the player attempts to use HERO_SWORD to attack Susie when she enters the room, Kris will pull her away. Kris is also shown on multiple occasions to enjoy staring at Susie.[103]

Ralsei

Kris and Ralsei work together throughout Deltarune, including several times the two are separated from the rest of the party. Ralsei appears to be aware of the SOUL's influence on Kris and encourages them to close their eyes and think of something else, in order to be able to speak to them freely without the player listening in. Ralsei generally likes Kris very much and sees them as a friend, offering to help them with puzzles and complimenting them.

Kris appears to be indifferent to Ralsei at first. However, they show support for him in later Chapters, even without the player's input. It is implied that Kris likes Ralsei less than Susie in Chapter 2, as the Ralsei Tea restores less HP for them than the Susie Tea. However, as the story progresses, Kris occasionally shows some kindness towards Ralsei, such as laying flat on the ground next to him when prompted by the player to "show him compassion" after he is stepped on by Susie during a scene in Chapter 4. Additionally, in the opposite situation, Kris will occasionally cover their mouth or cough mid-sentence when the player picks a dialogue option that would upset Ralsei.[104] As with Susie, Kris looks away from the screen when prompted to kill HERO_SCARF in the Original Game.

It is unclear what exactly Kris's feelings are towards Ralsei, as most of their interactions take place with the player controlling Kris, and it is never revealed what the two do during moments when the player does not see them.

Noelle Holiday

Kris and Noelle are neighbors and childhood friends. Years prior to the events of Deltarune, Noelle and Kris used to be close and spent a lot of time together, sometimes as a group with their older siblings. Kris had a reputation for pulling pranks on Noelle, but was often swiftly reprimanded by Dess for it. However, the two began to grow apart over time and Kris became somewhat of a loner without her.

In Chapter 1, Noelle offers to let Kris join her group for the project and later asks if they need help with homework, showing that she has remained helpful to them even after they grew apart.

In Chapter 2, Kris and Noelle end up forming a truce and traversing Cyber City together. There, Noelle reminisces about their childhood and opens up to Kris, slowly rekindling their friendship. One of the Addisons asks if the two of them are friends or something else, and Noelle is happy when "We're friends" is chosen.[105] Noelle also mentions that Kris is the only person who knows how weird she is,[106] and that Kris and she know each other better than anyone,[107] indicating a deep familiarity between the two of them.

In Chapter 4, Noelle is surprised when Kris compliments her singing and mentions that she misses hearing them play the piano.[108] While at her house, Kris automatically steals one of Noelle's pencils out of habit, which she doesn't react to, presumably being used to it. When eavesdropping on her and Susie in Dess's room, Noelle mentions Kris's odd behavior[109] and voice in the last few days, making her one of the only people who noticed a change in Kris since the player took control.

Weird Route

Following the Weird Route puts a considerable strain on Noelle's and Kris's relationship, as she perceives the player's choices to be made by Kris.

In Chapter 2's Weird Route, Noelle gradually becomes more scared of Kris because of the violent orders given to her, and even considers harming them at one point,[110] before seemingly falling into a trance-like state where she actively wants to become stronger with Kris's help. After waking up in the Light World, Noelle initially thinks what happened in the Cyber World was just a dream, but becomes genuinely terrified when Kris is made to corner her at the hospital while wearing her wristwatch.

In Chapter 4's Weird Route, Noelle is focused on Kris rather than Susie when she invites them to her house, and asks Kris to speak with her in private once there. She reveals that Kris visited her, comforted her and apologized, before removing a thorn from her finger. Like in the normal route, she is able to differentiate the player's voice from Kris's, as Noelle mentions feeling relieved when she heard Kris's regular voice. She is shocked and becomes terrified of Kris again when the player reads her thoughts and re-equips the Thorn Ring to her. Kris gives the player multiple options to abort the route, flees the room with Noelle if they spot the SOUL early, and reacts violently towards the SOUL if the route is continued, showing they are very protective of Noelle. Despite everything, Noelle seems excited to see Kris at the festival by the end of the day.[111]

Berdly

Kris and Berdly have a rivalry with each other, but are implied to have played video games together in the past (especially fighting games, by Berdly's mentions of wavedashing).[66] When selecting who they would like to go to the festival with, if Berdly is selected, then Kris repeats his name loudly.[112] They become allies throughout the later parts of Chapter 2 and work together to defeat Queen, with Berdly saving Kris, Susie, and Ralsei before their fight with GIGA Queen. Although Berdly believes the events in the Cyber World to be a dream after waking up, his opinions of Kris and Susie slightly soften, giving studying with them a chance and even nicely wishing them both a good day before leaving the Library.

December Holiday

As children, Kris and Asriel used to spend time with Noelle and Dess. It is implied that they were friends, although Dess may have been much closer to Asriel than she was to Kris. It seems Dess did not tolerate Kris's tormenting of Noelle, as she on at least one occasion smacked Kris with a wiffle bat when they were lying to Noelle.[113] It appears that Dess used to discipline Kris in this way often, as hinted at by Noelle's dialogue,[114] as well as Kris's reaction to some of the things in Dess's room.[115]

Rudolph Holiday

Rudy is a friend of Kris's father, Asgore. He affectionately sees Kris as "the creepy kid next door" he once yelled at for getting tangled in the Holidays' light display.[116] Rudy treats Kris kindly regardless, and jests if they inspect other objects in the room.[117] In Chapter 1, Rudy is surprised to see Kris visit him, and assumes Toriel urged them.

Spamton

On a normal route in Chapter 2, Kris first encounters Spamton in a Cyber City alleyway, making a deal to help him build his battle body by opening the basement in Queen's Mansion using KeyGen and carrying the disk. Kris seems scared of him, as they stagger backwards in each encounter, yet until the Spamton NEO battle on a normal route, Kris chooses to interact with him alone and vice-versa. It is in the Spamton NEO battle that the SOUL turns yellow. If his strings are cut in battle, his faith in the heroes increases as he turns into the Dealmaker, as well as giving Kris a Shadow Crystal. When Susie asks if Kris is okay after the battle, if the player selects "No," Kris turns away and yells. Otherwise, they are left with goosebumps, a grumbling stomach, and are strained to say anything, implying Spamton's words about being a puppet, combined with his ultimate fate, deeply unsettled Kris.

On the Weird Route, he makes his initial deal with Noelle and Kris to sell them the Thorn Ring. During his battle, he expresses disappointment in Kris for using violence on him and feels betrayed.

Spamton also seems aware of Kris's strained relationship to their SOUL, saying they are "the same," referring to the strings that control him.

Tenna

Kris was one of Tenna's most frequent users, watching him with their family and the Holidays and playing games with Asriel and Noelle. They, with Susie and Ralsei, become his contestants on TV Time during Chapter 3. As with the rest of the party, Tenna attempts to appeal to Kris's nostalgia to keep himself plugged in. Without completing the Sword Route, Kris deals less damage to Tenna during their battle.

During the Sword Route, Tenna is the final character they must kill to reach LV MAX. Before dying, he nervously states that he had followed his side of their deal and asks whether they will turn him off again.[118]

Knight

Kris's relationship with the Knight is unclear. During the party's battle with the Knight in Chapter 3, Kris deals less damage than any other party member unless Ralsei and Susie are DOWNed, in which case their damage output increases. This implies that while Kris is holding back against the Knight, they do not tolerate it harming their friends.

In Chapter 4, if Kris is able to reach the Knight in the Dark Sanctuary, they do not attack it despite Susie's insistence. When Ralsei asks Kris to close their eyes, if they do not choose to picture Susie, they are given the choice to think of the Knight. When Kris imagines it removing its helmet, they vividly imagine Jockington and Temmie instead to avoid thinking of the Knight's identity.[119]

Creation and Development

One of earliest concepts art of Kris is from 2014 or 2015, as which was shared by Toby Fox on his official Twitter on 2018.[120]

Gigi helped with Kris's character design, and has also created the various alternate costumes for Kris.[121] Chess worked on the Dark World color palette[54] and had created some base sprites and animations for Temmie Chang to work on.[122] One of the animations Chess has created includes Kris's idle animation during encounters.

During the UNDERTALE 6th Anniversary stream, Toby Fox has some comments during the modified Chapter 1. Further in the stream, Toby states Kris's pronouns.[52]

At the start of the game, Toby states that the scene of Susie menacing Kris is inspired by a real bully in his life, who was familiar with his mother.[123]

During the start of ??????, Toby confirms that buttons can be mashed to make Kris stand up sooner. He describes it as a "pointless detail."[85] During the spade storm sequence, Kris is set to automatically run, as Toby was unsure if it was possible to finish the sequence by walking through it, and if players would be aware of the running feature due to it not being present in Undertale.[86]

ACTs

You can do many things. Don't confuse it with magic.

Chapter 2

As a human, Kris cannot cast spells. Instead, Kris uses the ACT command, which allows them to perform actions that satisfy the enemy and make them possible to spare. These actions are different for each enemy.

Most ACTs do not require TP to perform, although there are some ACTs that do cost TP. Certain ACTs are exclusive to Kris and cost TP, making them similar to spells, although these are still implied not to be magic. These include:

  • Courage versus King,
  • Pirouette versus Jevil,
  • HealDeal versus Spamton.
  • X-Slash and FriedPipis versus Spamton NEO on the Weird Route.
  • Banish versus Titan Spawn.
  • Unleash versus Jackenstein and the Titan.

In the fights with King and Queen in Chapter 1 and Chapter 2, as well as in the battle against the Titan in Chapter 4, Kris can ACT with Susie, Ralsei, and the old man to conjure improved versions of their spells, such as Dual Heal and Red Buster. These spells are stated in-game to be enhanced by the SOUL "shining its power" upon the other party members.

In Chapter 2, Ralsei states that ACTing is Kris's "unique talent" as opposed to casting spells.[124] However, shortly after, Susie, Ralsei and Noelle gain the ability to ACT during their own turns via S-Action, R-Action or N-Action. Kris's ability to ACT still retains some advantages over them, though, as they have access to multiple ACTs per enemy (as opposed to one) and they can ACT together with other party members to achieve more powerful effects.

X-Slash

Costs 25% TP. Only usable against Spamton NEO during the Weird Route. An attack that hits the enemy twice with delayed strikes in a single turn, doing moderately more damage than the standard Fight action. Deals damage based on Kris's Attack, rounded half-up ( ( A t t a c k × 7 . 5 ) − ( T a r g e t D e f e n s e × 3 ) ) × 1 . 2 5 .[125] In Chapter 2, X-Slash can deal a maximum of 558 damage total (279 damage per slash) with the Mecha Saber, Jevilstail, and a Iron Shackle equipped against Spamton NEO (without accounting for stat bonuses from defeating enemies).

Trivia

  • Kris is exclusively referred to using singular "they/them" pronouns. This suggests that Kris is non-binary.
    • Kris is also a gender-neutral name, applicable to anyone regardless of gender.
    • When using the "TakeCare" or "TakeCareX" ACT against Virovirokun, Kris can appear with either a doctor's headband, a nurse's cap, or neither. This is unique to them, as all other characters only have one possible outfit.
  • Kris bears a resemblance to an unused human sprite from Undertale. Albeit, this is primarily due to the shadowed face, the identical hair color, and the similar shirt coloring.
  • Kris's Dark World appearance resembles Varik from the Brandish series, whom Toby Fox used as the protagonist for the EarthBound Halloween Hack. The Brandish series itself has also been used as an inspiration for Deltarune and other projects by Toby Fox.
  • Kris shares several characteristics with Frisk and Chara from Undertale:
    • Similarly to Frisk, both use singular "they" as a personal pronoun, albeit Chara distinctly goes by "it" when reflecting in third person.
    • Kris has a similar name to Frisk, and has the exact skin and hair colors. Their Dark World mantle's palette is also reminiscent of Frisk's shirt.[126] Both also exclusively go by singular "they" pronouns.
      • Clam Girl discusses "Suzy", her neighbor's daughter who appears to be Frisk's age, and suggests that Suzy should be Frisk's friend. This may be a parallel to Kris and Susie's friendship in Deltarune.
    • Like Chara, Kris enjoys chocolates, appears to have some interest in knives, is adopted by the Dreemurr family, is good friends with their brother Asriel, and wears a green-yellow striped shirt.
  • Pressing the cancel key when Kris gets out of bed at the beginning of a Chapter causes them to leave their bed faster.
  • Kris is the only party member to not have a unique hurt sprite when they receive damage in battle.
  • Kris starts out with a max HP of 90 when first entering the Dark World, and their max HP increases as each Fountain is sealed. However, their maximum amount of HP in the Light World is always 20 - the same amount as Frisk at LV 1.
  • Kris's red eye flash in the ending of Chapter 1 is reused from Undertale, as its sprite names (spr_asgore_eyeflash_serious) confirm that it is the same as Asgore's in his boss fight.
  • Kris's dance during Chapter 2's Sweet Cap'n Cakes fight and Chapter 3's Rouxls Kaard fight takes inspiration from the Slavic dance "Kozachok," more commonly known as the "Squat Dance" or "Kazotsky Kick" outside of Slavic communities.
  • When Susie thinks about what Kris would do during the Ferris wheel scene in Chapter 2, one of the options is "Point and hearts come out," referring to her and Kris's ACT animation. As of Chapter 3, this now also can refer to how, in the Wild West Minigame and the Tenna boss fight, when Kris wants to shoot something, they point with a finger gun and heart-shaped bullets are fired.
